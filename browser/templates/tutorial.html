How to Buy Tokens

This article will teach you how to buy ERC20 tokens on the Ethereum platform
There are several ways to purchase crowdsale tokens (and interact with the Ethereum blockchain.). Unfortunately it is no longer possible to do so on exchanges, but many of the alternatives below are more secure and just as easy. Scroll down to review the different methods, or choose one from the list:

Step 1: Install a token wallet

You must not attempt to purchase tokens with an exchange wallet! At this time exchanges do not support the Ethereum ERC20 tokens, so there is no way to trade or use coins purchased with an exchange account. You need to transfer your funds to a private wallet first.

We present four options below for generating your own personal wallet. Metamask is simplest and most recommended method.

  • Metamask
  • Mist
  • Parity
  • MyEtherWallet

Metamask Browser Plugin

Metamask is a browser plugin that enables websites to propose Ethereum transactions on your behalf. Once you confirm the transaction, it is submitted directly to the blockchain.

Metamask works best with Google Chrome, but they also provide versions for Firefox and Opera.

We notice you aren't using a Metamask compatible browser. We recommend downloading Google Chrome then installing Metamask here.

MyEtherWallet

MyEtherWallet is a website that lets you easily create private Ethereum wallets. Since they allow exporting private keys, you fully own the wallet and can easily manage tokens. This method is slightly simpler than Metamask but far less secure. If MyEtherWallet is compromised or you visit a scam site (always double check the url), your wallet funds can be irreversibly stolen.

That being said, MyEtherWallet is still a good way to manage your wallet if used properly. Visit MyEtherWallet.com to create a new wallet.

Mist Wallet

Mist is a desktop app that provides an Ethereum wallet and other Ethereum related functionality. Mist can be downloaded here, run the file to install.

Mist runs an Ethereum node on your computer that connects to other nodes in the Ethereum network. It receives new blocks that have been mined on the network, and verifies them using the entire history of the blockchain. Therefore it uses a lot of disk space (tens of GB), so only run Mist if you have more than enough available.

Parity

Parity is an Ethereum client that runs in the browser, but must be downloaded locally. It comes with an advanced wallet manager and many other interesting features. Parity can be downloaded here, install it how you would any program (just open it).

Parity is similar to Mist in that it takes a lot of disk space, since it is running a light version of a full Ethereum node. It has features for advanced users and developers, so we only recommend it if you plan to use more then just the wallet feature.

Step 2: Purchase 'ether' from an exchange

Your new wallet will start out with 0 ether (Ethereum's unit of currency). Before purchasing crowdsale tokens you need to get some ether from an exchange. If you already have an account with an exchange that supports Ethereum, you can skip to Step 3.

An easy and safe way to buy Ethereum is through Coinbase. You can check whether it is available in your country here, and sign up for an account here. Once you create and verify an account, add a payment method such as credit card or direct bank transfer.

Note that while it is usually fast to verify accounts from major banks, it may take 3-5 business days for Coinbase to receive transfers. Credit and debit card transfers are instant but incur a higher fee (1.49% for bank, 3.99% for debit/credit). Your bank may also charge an extra fee for credit card transactions. You can learn more about Coinbase fees here.

If Coinbase is not available in your country we recommend you research which exchange is best for you. There are many options, you can find a list sorted by volume here.

Once you have an exchange account with a verified payment method, simply follow their instructions to buy ether! You can use the calculator on our token page to figure out how much ether you need.

Step 3: Transfer ether to your new wallet

If you purchased ether on Coinbase, log in to your account and click the Accounts tab. Find your ETH Wallet on the left and click Send. Enter the address of your private wallet created in Step 1, enter the amount you wish to transfer, and confirm the transaction. After a few minutes the transaction should be confirmed by the blockchain and the ether will be available in your private wallet.

If you purchased ether on a different exchange, follow their instructions for sending ether.

Step 4: Purchase tokens

Troubleshooting

+ I transferred ether from Coinbase to Metamask, but it hasn't appeared!

Log in to Coinbase and verify that you sent to the correct address!

During periods of congestion normal transactions may take a long time. Visit etherscan.io and enter your transaction id on the top right to track it.

+ I installed Mist/Parity, but it stopped working or froze my computer!

Mist and Parity download a compressed version of the full blockchain, so make sure you have at least 30GB free on your computer. We highly recommend using Metamask instead, because the blockchain download is not required.
